⚙️ Scorecard Methodology

🔄 Transaction Behavior (25%)

Monthly payment volume, average ticket size, active days per month, and growth trend. Higher volume with consistent activity and positive growth indicates stable, expanding cashflow.

Volume percentile, Active days %, Growth trend, Ticket size

⚠️ Risk Ratios (30%)

Refund ratio and chargeback ratio are strongest predictors of merchant quality. High chargebacks (>2%) trigger automatic risk flags. Refund ratio >15% indicates product/service quality issues.

Refund ratio penalty, Chargeback ratio penalty

💵 Financial Health (25%)

DSCR (Debt Service Coverage Ratio), settlement history score, GST filing regularity, and bank inflow consistency vs. reported volume. DSCR >2.0x indicates healthy debt coverage.

DSCR, Settlement score, GST score, Inflow consistency

📈 Stability & Maturity (20%)

Business vintage on platform, seasonality index (stable cashflow preferred), entity type (Pvt Ltd vs. Proprietorship), and website quality score. Vintage >24 months signals business maturity.

Vintage, Seasonality, Entity type, Website score

🤖 k-Means Clustering

Unsupervised learning segments merchants into 4 risk clusters using 10 behavioral and financial features. Clusters are mapped to grades A-D based on average risk score within each cluster.

Algorithm: scikit-learn KMeans (k=4)

💳 Credit Limit Engine

Limit = 50% of monthly volume × grade multiplier (A=3x, B=2x, C=1x, D=0.3x) × DSCR adjustment (capped at 2x). Hard cap at ₹500 lakhs. Grade A merchants with DSCR >2.0 get highest limits.

Dynamic pricing based on risk + capacity
💡 About This Project

This project simulates how fintech lenders like Razorpay Capital assess working-capital eligibility for SMEs using alternative data from payment platforms rather than traditional audited financials. It bridges my background in corporate credit appraisal (CIBIL, FOIR, DSCR, CAM reports) with fintech's cashflow-based underwriting approach.

Key differentiators: (1) Uses transaction velocity, chargeback patterns, and settlement history as credit proxies instead of balance sheets. (2) Combines rule-based scorecards with unsupervised clustering for robust risk segmentation. (3) Generates actionable recommendations (Auto-Approve → Manual Review → Reject) with explainable risk factors. (4) Credit limit engine dynamically adjusts for risk grade and repayment capacity (DSCR). (5) Built entirely with synthetic data to demonstrate the model architecture without exposing real merchant information.
